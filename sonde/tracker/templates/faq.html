Sonde Nepal FAQ

Radiosonde Tracking System in Nepal

General Questions

What is a radiosonde and how does Sonde Nepal work?
A radiosonde is a small instrument attached to a weather balloon. It measures temperature, humidity, pressure, wind, and GPS location while ascending up to ~30 km.

We receive signals using SDR, decode them, and display real-time data.
Which radiosonde is used in Nepal?
Commonly iMet-54 or similar models operating in 400–406 MHz.
Where is the station and launch time?
Kirtipur (Tribhuvan University). Usually around 00 UTC daily.

Reception & Hardware

What antenna should I use?
Quarter-wave (~403 MHz) is enough. Yagi or collinear gives better range.
What SDR is recommended?
RTL-SDR (V3 or better). Add LNA for better results.
How does auto_rx work?
It scans the band, detects signals, locks frequency, and decodes telemetry automatically.

Technical

What frequencies are used?
400.1 – 406 MHz (varies per launch).
What data is transmitted?
GPS, altitude, speed, temperature, humidity, diagnostics.
Range?
Up to 300–400 km line-of-sight.

Contribution

How can I contribute?
Set up a station, contribute code, share data, or help expand coverage.
Can I recover a radiosonde?
Yes. Use last GPS location. Follow safety and legal rules.

Troubleshooting

Weak signal?
Improve antenna, use LNA, check frequency and SDR calibration.
Is it legal?
Receiving is generally allowed. Transmitting is regulated.
