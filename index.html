What is a Radiosonde?

A radiosonde is a small electronic device attached to a weather balloon. It is sent into the sky to measure atmospheric data like temperature, humidity, and air pressure.

As the balloon rises, it sends this data back to ground stations using radio signals. This helps scientists and weather services understand the atmosphere and improve forecasts.

Why Tracking?

Tracking radiosondes allows us to follow the path of the balloon in real time. It helps in studying wind patterns and recovering the device after it lands.
